July-September 1993

Highlights

During July, August and September 1993, the following features were noticeable:

Surface winds were still strongest (more than 10 m/s) at 50S in the southern Indian Ocean and south of Austrialia. A well defined Anticyclonic circulation, associated with "blocking action", is seen in the northern Atlantic in September.

  • The longest drifting buoys trajectories are found at 60S. A "cluster" of near stationary buoys is found in the vicinity of the Anticyclonic center defined above.
  • Mean wave height greater than 4.5m are found in the southern hemisphere at 50S, while mean wave height greater than 3m are located south of the Labrador Sea and east of the Canadian trough which persisted in July and August.
  • The negative values of the SOI mentioned in the previous Bulletin persisted [ -1.0; -1.5; -0.7] even if SLP at bouth Darwin and Tahiti were above normal in August and September. Weak easterlies were found over the eastern tropical Pacific in September.
  • SSTA started to decrease in the eastern and central equatorial Pacific during July and August. However, a 0.5C anomaly appeared in the central equatorial Pacific during the last month. Accordingly convective activity increased near the date line.
  • The east Australian current and the Kuroshio Current and Extension remained anomalously cool.
  • Average sub-surface temperature anomaly [0/400m] are positive in the west Pacific and near the center of the north Atlantic gyre. The thermocline has deepened in the central equatorial Pacific near 140W, with evidence of easterly propagation during September.
While ENSO presented some signs of "relief" last June, the even continued well into the Summer. Atmospheric El-Niño like conditions re-developed near the date line in September with, in particular, a well formed sub-tropical jet. Blocking action over the northern Atlantic was present.

In this issue, the new analysis of the TOPEX-POSEIDON data (sea level deviation) is displayed. The ERS-1 blended analysis is resumed. We also include the quarterly mean SST and winds from the TOGA-TOA array in the equatorial Pacific. A new SST analysis in the tropical Atlantic from the "OPERA" numerical model is also displayed.
