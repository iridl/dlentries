April-June 1996

HIGHLIGHTS

During April, May and June of 1996, the following features were noticeable:
  • Unusual wet weather over Eastern Africa ( Ethiopia, Kenya and Tanzania ), in the Gulf of Guinea and Central and Western US. During June dry conditions are found in Central South America and Southeast Australia.
  • In the northern Atlantic drifting buoys are moving quite fast between 50°N and 60°N and 30°W. Long trajectories are also found along the path of the Pacific North Equatorial Current (NEC).
  • The SOI is positive with highest increase during June (+1.5). This value similar to values during 1988 is associated with above average trades over the Pacific. Nevertheless there is no evidence of La Nina in the SST anomalies while the strongest anticyclonic anomalies are found in the southern pacific south of 30 °S and centered around 120°W.
    soi standardized sea level pressure difference
  • Maximum sea level anomalies (above 15 cm, pressure corrected) are found around 10°S and the dateline and around 7°N and 135°E.
  • Maximum negative SST anomalies (-2°C) are found in the Kuroshio extension and could be associated with low frequency variability (decadal type) in this region. Maximum positive SSTA are found along the West African coastline (+1°C), in the equatorial Atlantic (+1.5°C) and southeast Indian ocean. This is a necessary condition for easterly wave amplification during summertime in the northern tropical Atlantic.
    IGOSS nmc monthly Sea Surface Temperature Anomaly May1996 (degreeC)
  • The 20°C isotherm is shoaling along the equatorial Pacific at 100°W during June. Coldest subsurface temperature anomalies (less than -2.5°C) are found along the equator at 50m deep from 80°W until 110°W. Warmest subsurface temperature anomalies (+2.5°C) are found west of the dateline at 120m deep approximately.
    IGOSS leetma depth of the 20C isotherm (m)
The above conditions in the Pacific are still associated with weak cold oceanic conditions. This should contribute to a weak enhancement of Atlantic Hurricanes in conjunction with above normal tropical Atlantic SSTA.
