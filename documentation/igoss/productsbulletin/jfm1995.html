January-March 1995

Highlights

During January, February and March of 1995, the following features were noticeable:
  • A blocking high persisted in the troposphere over Alaska from the end of February until the end of March. The previously well organized cyclonic circulation in the vicinity of the Aleutians became much weaker during March.
  • The longest trajectories of the drifting buoys were found in the western equatorial Pacific as well as in the northern Atlantic where the westerlies were quite strong.
  • The SOI which had been negative for the 10 consecutive months became positive in March. North easterlies were well established in the tropical Pacific while easterly anomalies appeared at the end of the period in the eastern Pacific. Westerlies persisted at 50°S, in the southern Indian Ocean and south of Australia. Strong westerlies were evident between 50°N and 60°N in the northern Pacific and Atlantic Oceans
  • Mean wave height above 4.5m were found between 40°N and 60°N again in the northern Pacific and Atlantic Ocean.
  • Positive SSTA (+ 1°C) persisted in the central Pacific Ocean while negative anomalies appeared in the eastern Pacific during March. During the same month negative SSTA prevailed in the north Pacific around 30°N.
    March 1995 Pacific SSTA
  • Cold heat storage in the upper 400m (HS 400) is now found along the equatorial Pacific. During March warmer HS 400 appeared in the western Indian Ocean as well as the northern Atlantic Ocean. these are classic conditions of an anti El Nino period. This is well demonstrated in movies available on the World Wide Web at http://cyberia.ucsd.edu/WARREN/. An homogeneous pressure corrected sea-level pressure anomaly (over 5 cm) appeared in February and lasted in march at 10°S and around the dateline.
    March-April 1995 Heat Storage Anomalies
The March oceanic conditions reflect the end of the matured warm phase of the global ENSO signal. Warm oceanic conditions are to be expected during the year in the western tropical and northern Atlantic Ocean (Lau and Nath, 1994, J. Climate, 7, 1184-1207; Tourre and White, 1995, JPO, 25, 1317-1332).
