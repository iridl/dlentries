January-March 1996

Highlights

During January and February of 1996, the following features were noticeable:
  • Southeast Africa (Zimbabwe, eastern Zambia, Natal and Transvaal) southeast Brazil and western Madagascar were extremely wet. Wet and cold weather dominated over eastern US, while wet conditions also prevailed over northern California, southwest Oregon and central Washington. Southwestern Europe and northwestern Africa were warm and wet.
  • In the northern Atlantic drifting buoys are still caught in small scales oceanic eddies. The longest trajectories are found at 5°S all along the Pacific ocean and along the circumpolar current.
  • The SOI is slightly positive (anomalies still below those of May 1990). Easterly are well established over the central Pacific with positive anomalies there.
    Standardized SOI
  • Sea level anomalies (pressure corrected) are building up west of the dateline.
  • Sea surface temperature anomalies (SSTA) were above normal northeast and southwest of Australia. The Australian monsoon could nevertheless be classified as moderate. Two tropical cyclones (Doloresse and Flossy) formed in the Indian ocean. The subtropical southern Atlantic is warmer than normal. Negative SSTA (-1°C) were found around 155°W during January and expanded at the equator from 130°W to the dateline during February.
    IGOSS nmc monthly Sea Surface Temperature Anomaly Feb1996 (degreeC)
  • The thermocline continued to deepen in the western and central Pacific (-20 m), the zero-line anomaly being located around 150°W and the equator.
    IGOSS leetma depth of the equatorial 20C isotherm (m)
The above conditions in the Pacific can be associated with weak La Niña period. This is particularly true for the in-phase negative SSTA anomalies at 35°N and in the central and eastern Equatorial Pacific. It is also remarkable to note that the entire tropical Atlantic is warmer than normal and positive SSTA are also present at the southern tip of south Africa.
