January-March 1994

Highlights

During January, February and March 1994, the following features were noticeable:

Surface winds were strongest [more than 10 m/s] in the Southern Oceans between 50S and 60S, as well as in the northern Atlantic between 40N and 60N where strong anticyclonic circulation was present. In the tropical Pacific the easterlies increased in January and February, the SPCZ being displaced south-westward. The Australian monsoon was very active during February.

  • The clustering of the drifting buoys around 30N persisted in the Atlantic during January and February.
  • A negative PNA took place in February, the SOI being near zero or slightly negative during the period.
  • Equatorial SSTA were slightly positive in the western Pacific and near zero in the eastern Pacific. The southeast sector of the Warm Pool was colder than normal in March. The coldest SST were found south of Greenland during March.
  • During February and March the depth of the equatorial Pacific thermocline was near normal in the west and slightly shallower in the east.
  • Positive heat storage anomalies were found in the Pacific and north-Atlantic subtropical gyres. Negative anomalies were found along the Pacific and Atlantic ITCZ.
In this issue, we resume the publication of the SST in the tropical Atlantic from France's Opera model.
