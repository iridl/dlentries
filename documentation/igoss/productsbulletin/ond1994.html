October-December 1994

Highlights

During October, November and December of 1994, the following features were noticeable:
  • There was very high SLP over Indonesia and Australia conducive of rainfall deficit and droughts in those regions. A strong atmospheric anticyclonic circulation is present in the northern Pacific with a reversed PNA pattern in November. Winds larger than 10m/s were found during December in the northern Pacific and Atlantic Oceans as well as in the Southern Oceans between Tasmania and Antarctica.
  • The longest trajectories of the drifting buoys were found in the Pacific section of the circumpolar current as well as off the Japanese coastline and around 20!N and 150!W [December].
  • The SOI was strongly negative during the entire period and for the 8th consecutive month. Weak easterlies were found near the dateline with westerly anomalies larger than 8m/s and actual westerlies during November.
  • Mean wave height above 5m were found In the northern Pacific and Atlantic Oceans [December]. Values over 4m were associated with the circumpolar current particularly south of Australia.
  • SST anomalies [larger than 2C] appear at the Equator between the dateline and 160W. Actual temperatures greater than 30C are found also near the dateline. Large SST anomalies dissipated in the northern Atlantic Ocean while they appeared in the northern Pacific at 35N between 150W and 175W [ December].
    December 1994 Pacific SST Anomalies
  • The thermocline depth increased in the central and eastern Pacific. The 20C isotherm is found 50m deeper between 120!W and the dateline. Warm heat storage in the upper 400m is found in the eastern equatorial Pacific as well as the central Indian Oceans. The equatorial sea-level anomaly was positive on the Equator at the dateline during Fall [+4cm] the largest anomalies of +8cm and -8cm being located in the central and eastern Indian Ocean respectively. Negative values of -8cm were found at 150W and 12N and positive values of +8cm off the coast of Japan.
    Nov-Dec 1994 Heat Storage Anomaly
The December oceanic conditions reflect the matured warm phase of ENSO, with the global heat storage anomalies consistent with the canonical pattern determined by Tourre and White [to appear in JPO, June 1995].
