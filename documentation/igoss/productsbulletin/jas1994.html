July-September 1994

Highlights

During July, August and September 1994, the following features were noticeable:
  • There was a strong summer monsoon circulation over the Indian Ocean during July and August. A maximum pseudo-stress anomaly of 80m2 s-2 occurred during August 10S and 60E. The anti-cyclonic circulation in the northern Pacific and Atlantic oceans were well established during July. Winds larger than 10m s-1 were found during the entire period at 50S from the tip of South Africa to 120oE.
  • The longest trajectories of the drifting buoys were found at 50oS between 30E and 90E.
  • The SOI was strongly negative during the entire period [-1.8] and for the 5th consecutive month.
  • Mean wave height above 4.5m were found between 30S and 60S and from 30E to 150E during July and August. Values larger than 5m were found during August south of Tasmania.
  • A patch of SST anomalies [larger than 1C] not only persists at the Equator and the dateline but extends northeastward during September. Large SST anomalies were also found at 40N in the northwest Pacific and Atlantic Oceans. It is noteworthy that the southern oceans are also anomalously warm during September. The coldest sea-surface temperature was located where the "warm pool" is usually found, and also in the southwest Pacific.
  • The thermocline deepened in the central and eastern Pacific. The sea-level anomaly was positive on the Equator at the dateline during September [+8cm]. Negative values were found in the eastern equatorial Pacific and at 10N and 150W.
A continuation of the Pacific warm condition combined with the anomalous pattern described above could lead to the formation of a full-fledged warm ENSO episode.
