Integrated Global Ocean Services System Products Bulletin
Introduction

Editor's Note

This is the first Electronic version of the quarterly IGOSS Products Bulletin [E-IPB]. IGOSS is a joint working committee between the Intergovernmental Oceanographic Commission (IOC) and the World Meterological Organization (WMO). Its continuing evolution is being implemented at the Lamont-Doherty Earth Observatory [LDEO] of Columbia University, in response to the needs of the oceanographic and earth science communities for real-time Oceanography. The format of the global analyses is standardized. In addition, the different data sets have been made available at grid points. This will allow users to edit, contour and compute their preferred indices at diverse locations.

This new format assures that accurate data-sets are made available in a timely fashion. It fully supports programs and service-systems such as the Global Ocean Observing System [GOOS], the Global Climate Observing System [GCOS], the Climate Variability Program [CLIVAR], and the newly begun Global Ocean Atmosphere Land System [GOALS].

The "Bundesamt für Seeschiffart und Hydrographie" [BSH] in Hamburg, will continue to print and distribute the hard-copy version of the IPB, worldwide. We hope that the electronic version will be a useful tool for our community. The Internet addresses of the different analysts and national services involved in the IPB are included to ease communications between users and servers.

Our thanks are directed to Dr. Benno Blumenthal at LDEO, whose collaboration in the making of the E-IPB is deeply appreciated.

Please direct any comments or suggestions to tourre@ldeo.columbia.edu.

Yves M. Tourre, Ph.D.
