April-June 1995

Highlights

During April, May and June of 1995, the following features were noticeable:
  • Drifting buoys stalled in the Atlantic Ocean around 30°N.
  • The SOI dropped sharply during April, due to below average SLP at Tahiti. It then re-increased during the following 2 months but was still slightly negative at the end of June. Weak easterly anomalies were found across the central equatorial pacific at the end of the period.
  • Mean wave height above 4 m were found at 45°N in both the northwest Pacific and Atlantic Oceans during April and May. 3m mean wave height are apparent along Somalia during June. The highest mean wave persisted southwest of Australia during June.
  • SST returned to normal in June in the tropical Pacific and Indian Oceans. Cool waters are in the extreme eastern pacific Ocean (-0.5°C; -1°C anomalies). Negative SSTA are still found in the north Pacific at 40°N (-1°C anomalies), while positive SSTA (+2°C) are found in the eastern- northeastern Atlantic Ocean.
    June 1995 SSTA
  • The thermocline is getting deeper in the western equatorial Pacific. By and large the heat storage in the upper 400m (HS 400) along the equatorial Pacific is near average.
The most conspicuous feature during that period is the large SSTA (+2°C) in the eastern and northeastern Atlantic Ocean (May and June). This associated with a northeastward displacement of the Azores High (+7 mb at 50°N).
