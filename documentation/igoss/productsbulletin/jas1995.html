July-September 1995

Highlights

During July, August and September of 1995, the following features were noticeable:
  • Extreme dry conditions over western Europe and persisting hot weather over northwest Africa. The maximum SLP anomalies (over 5mb) were found over Scotland and the northeast Pacific during the month of August. The northeast displacement of the Azores High had already been noticed in June. The strongest surface winds are in the Indian Ocean associated with the Summer monsoon and strong westerlies at 50°S.
  • At around 30°N in the Atlantic drifting buoys were caught in small scales oceanic eddies, westward motion dominated east of the Philippines, longest eastward trajectories were found between Australia and Antarctica and south of South Africa.
  • The SOI is weakly positive (+0.2) , weak easterly anomalies dominate over the central and eastern equatorial Pacific, while the ENSO indicators remained neutral in August.
    Standardized SOI
  • Mean wave height above 4 m were found in the Arabian sea while values over 4.5m were found in the Southern Ocean at 45°S (greatest values, 5m and above, located at 45°S between 60°E and 75°E).
  • During September negative SSTA re-appeared for the first time in 6 years in the Niño 4 area (5°N-5°S, 160°E-150°W) while positive SSTA dominated the western Pacific and the eastern Indian Ocean (+0.5°C).
    September 1995 SSTA in the Niño 4 area
    The most conspicuous feature is the persisting large positive SSTA in the eastern Atlantic ocean with anomalies of +1°C found at 20°N and 50°N. Positive SSTA in the path of African easterly waves as well as tropospheric Atlantic conditions, associated with the end of an El Niño, are factors contributing to enhanced Hurricane activity in the Atlantic (see Gray, et al 1995 Early August Hurricane Forecast, also Gray et al., Wea. Forecasting, 1993a and b).At the end of the period the largest negative SSTA are found in the central north Pacific around 40°N and the southwestern southern Atlantic around 50°S. The SST along the eastern USA is colder than normal.
    September 1995 Atlantic SSTA
  • The thermocline continue to get deeper in the western equatorial Pacific (from 130°E to 170°W). During July-August, homogeneous areas with positive heat storage anomalies (larger than 0.5 J/m2) were identify in the vicinity of the Pacific SPCZ and in the north tropical Atlantic, around 15°N.
