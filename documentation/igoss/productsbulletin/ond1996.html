October-December 1996

Highlights

During October, November and December of 1996, the following features were noticeable:
  • In a narrow band north of the equatorial Pacific drifting buoys are moving quite fast in the vicinity of a well defined NECC. On the contrary buoys are stalled in the Gulf of Alaska (November).
  • The SOI remained positive ( 0.8 in December). This is associated with easterly anomalies of the trades over the central and eastern Pacific and westerly anomalies from the eastern Indian Ocean to the Maritime Continent. Over these regions the convective activity is extremely intense. The intra-seasonal oscillation picked up during November and December. Over the northern Atlantic the NAO is reversed during December bringing cold air over western Europe and lots of precipitation over Spain and the Mediterranean basin.
  • Negative SST anomalies (-0.3° C to -0.8° C) are found east of the dateline in the Nino 3 area. Positive SSTA are found in the Kuroshio Extension and west of 170° E (+0.5° C) and along the SPCZ. At 60°S except in the Pacific the SST is 1°C above normal. The warmest parts of the Atlantic ocean in December are found at 40°N and 40°S at 40°W. The SSTA in the eastern Atlantic Ocean are above normal around 40° N. Subsurface temperature in the central and eastern equatorial Pacific Ocean are below normal.
  • The thermocline is extremely deep in the western Pacific during December (deepest levels of the last ten years) with sea level pressure corrected anomalies up to 16 cm.
The above conditions are associated with a slight intensification of a cold episode in the Pacific Ocean. The Walker circulation has been accordingly reinforced.
